Jeff Lynne and the Elecrtic Light Orchestra

Who is Jeff Lynne?

Born in December of 1947, Jeff Lynne is the founder and lead singer of Electric Light Orchestra (ELO). He started the band in 1970 and produced 15 studio albums with the band, plus 2 solo albums in 1990 and 2012. Lynne was heavily inspired by The Beatles, which is reflected in his distinct musical style.

My top 5 favorite ELO albums

  • 1. Out of the Blue

    The double LP released in October of 1977, Out of the Blue, was ELO’s 7th studio album. To this day, it is one of the best selling albums by the group reaching 10 million copies sold.

  • 2. A New World Record

    Released in October of 1976, A New World Record was the band’s 6th album. This album’s songs lean more towards shorter pop its, starting a trend for the rest of their discography.

  • 3. Discovery

    ELO’s 8th studio album, Discovery, was released in June of 1998. It became the band’s first albums to reach #1 in the UK, holding that position for 5 weeks.

  • 4. Face the Music

    Face the Music was ELO’s 5th studio released in September of 1975. This album began the shift to more radio-friendly pop music as opposed to the longer, more orchestral music from their previous album, Eldorado.

  • 5. Alone in the Universe

    Alone in the Universe was the first album to be produced solo by Jeff Lynne as his 13th studio album. The album released under the moniker “Jeff Lynne’s ELO” in response to tribute bands using “ELO” to promote their tours.
